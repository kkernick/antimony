lib
The Library Fabricator is most important of all, as it assembles the SOF. It also touches almost every other fabricator, and is the central part of the most important path between bin-library-syscalls. It is also by the far the most complicated, as libraries can encompass files, wildcards, directories, and binaries. They can be sourced from just about anywhere on the system (IE /usr/bin, or /usr/share/application), and it needs to determine which files should be placed in the SOF, and what to do with their dependencies. It relies on LDD to determine ELF dependencies (IE .so files), and Find to scour directories. Everything is aggressively cached, and even more aggressively parallelized.
